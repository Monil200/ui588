Massoud Ghyam

Massoud Ghyam has spent most of his professional life as a director and producer in film. Born and educated in USA, Los Angeles he grew up traveling the world, as part of a family that produced world syndicated television. Massoud went on to study and earn a business degree at University of Southern California and continued his studies in film and Photography at USC FAPH.

Massoud’s photography stems from an early passion for constructing emotional images that communicate a story. His imagery, both in landscapes and portraits, seeks to stir our awareness. Each photograph is an invitation to personal discovery and reflection that leaves us somehow changed.

Influenced by painters like Lyndon Wade, Steve McCury and Senol Zorlu, Massoud spent years studying and deconstructing composition through their works. His work has also been influenced by filmmakers Luigi Bussolati,Edlo Kawa and Cornelia Adams and their use of imagery to evoke emotion and tell a story.

Massoud has been a speaker most recently at PDN Photoplus in New York as well as the Leica Academy in Los Angeles, on the topic of “The Photo - That Moment”. He is represented by Faith Gallery, USA and Imagery Gallery, Spain.

All of Massoud’s exhibition images are silver gelatin prints from a negative.
